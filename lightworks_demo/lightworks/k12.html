To Students

Our K-12 Mindfulness Course includes separate textbooks for each grade, ensuring that content is age-appropriate and engaging. The current teachers at your school will deliver the mindfulness curriculum, creating a familiar and supportive learning environment. This integration helps reinforce mindfulness practices within the existing educational framework.

Timeline

Each grade level receives a dedicated 30-hour program spread across the school year. This ensures ample time for students to absorb and apply mindfulness practices effectively.

Delivery

The program is delivered through interactive classroom sessions by existing teachers of each grade, providing students with a hands-on experience in mindfulness. Teachers will guide students through practical exercises, discussions, and activities designed to make mindfulness a natural part of their daily lives.

Indian Government Initiatives for Mindfulness & Meditation in Schools

The Indian government is making significant strides towards integrating mindfulness into the education system, reflecting a growing recognition of its benefits for students' overall well-being and academic success. Recent initiatives and policies emphasize the importance of incorporating mindfulness practices into school curricula across the country.

National Education Policy (NEP) 2020

The NEP 2020 outlines a comprehensive framework for transforming the Indian education system, highlighting the need to promote positive mental health and emotional well-being among students. It encourages the inclusion of holistic education practices, including mindfulness, to foster a balanced and supportive learning environment.

School Mental Health Programs

In line with the NEP, the Ministry of Education has introduced various mental health initiatives aimed at improving students' emotional and psychological resilience. These programs advocate for the integration of mindfulness and emotional intelligence training into school activities and curriculums.

CBSE and State Education Boards' Guidelines

The Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) and several state education boards have issued guidelines recommending mindfulness and well-being programs as part of the school curriculum. These guidelines aim to create a structured approach to teaching mindfulness, ensuring that students benefit from consistent and evidence-based practices.

Pilot Programs and Grants

The government has launched pilot programs in select schools to test and refine mindfulness curriculums. These initiatives often come with grants and funding to support the development and implementation of mindfulness programs, providing resources for training teachers and integrating practices into daily school life.

Collaboration with NGOs and Educational Bodies

The Indian government collaborates with various non-governmental organizations and educational bodies to promote mindfulness and mental health education. These partnerships help in developing and disseminating effective mindfulness curriculums and providing training for educators.

By mandating mindfulness curriculums, the Indian government aims to address the growing need for emotional and mental health support in schools. These initiatives are designed to equip students with essential life skills, enhance their academic performance, and contribute to a healthier, more resilient generation.
